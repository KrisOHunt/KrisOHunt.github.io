Working After 50

It was a good plan. A really good plan. It just didn't work.

I was ready for my next career.

Before becoming a stay-at-home mom, a position I thought I'd never have, my career was mostly centered in marketing. Starting by pasting up the classified section of a newspaper and ending up as the head marketer for a local theatre where I did everything from emails, season ticket brochures, posters, newletters, and postcards to dressing up in costume and lunching with the Chamber of Commerce. When you're working with a limited budget, get ready to wear a silly hat.

After a 30 year career as a software engineer, Simon was ready to transition to his next stage in life. Call it his retirement career. He was ready to groom the next generation of clean coders by taking a position with the local community college.
